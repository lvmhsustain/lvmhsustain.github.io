Fun Facts

  • Each person in Japan produces an average of 356.2kg of waste per year and as a whole, Japan generates 45,360,000 tons of municipal waste per year, ranking 8th in the world
  • The "3R Initiative", launched in April 2005 by the Government of Japan, aims for the construction of a sound material-cycle society through the three "R's": "Reduce," "Reuse" and "Recycle."

Previous Strategies

  • To establish an environmental socio-economy, the Eco Town Program was created in 1997 with a Zero Emission Concept by shifting waste generated from one industry to be utilized as material in another industry in order to remove any waste.
  • My Hashi project: use your own hashi! Around a total of 24 billion pairs are used each year, which is equivalent to almost 200 pairs per person yearly.
  • Mottainai Furoshiki: the use of a furoshiki as a symbol of Japanese culture to reduce plastic bag waste
